Ahmad ibn Muhammad ibn Hajar al-Haytami studied in Cairo before relocating to Mecca, where he taught for most of his career and became one of the last towering authorities of the classical Shafi'i school. His commentary tradition — most importantly Tuhfat al-Muhtaj on Minhaj al-Talibin — is, alongside al-Ramli's Nihayat al-Muhtaj, one of the two pillars later scholars consult when determining the school's relied-upon position on a given issue. Where the two disagree, later students are often taught to follow whichever is more relevant to their region.
