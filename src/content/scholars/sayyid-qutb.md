Sayyid Qutb began his career as a literary critic before turning to religious writing; that background shows clearly in Fi Zilal al-Qur'an, which reads the Qur'an thematically and aesthetically rather than through the verse-by-verse legal and grammatical apparatus of most classical tafsirs. His broader political writings made him one of the most influential and contested figures in 20th-century Islamist thought.
