Al-Suyuti is remembered as one of history's most staggeringly productive scholars, reportedly authoring several hundred works across nearly every Islamic discipline. When his teacher Jalal al-Din al-Mahalli died having completed only part of a planned tafsir, al-Suyuti finished the remainder in a matching style so seamless that Tafsir al-Jalalayn is still read today as a single unified work rather than two authors' separate contributions.
