Ibn Raslan al-Ramli — not to be confused with the later al-Ramli family of Cairo — versified Ibn al-Barzi's prose legal text into rajaz meter, producing a poem long enough to be comprehensive but structured enough to memorize, spanning theology and spirituality alongside fiqh in a single continuous work.
