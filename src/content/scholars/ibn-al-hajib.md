Ibn al-Hajib worked across two disciplines at once, producing foundational advanced texts in both Nahw (al-Kafiyah) and Usul al-Fiqh (his Mukhtasar) that would each spawn centuries of commentary — al-Kafiyah in particular became a pillar of the Dars-e-Nizami curriculum still taught across South Asia.
