Taj al-Din al-Subki, son of the equally formidable Taqi al-Din al-Subki, wrote Jam' al-Jawami' as a deliberate synthesis of over a hundred earlier Usul al-Fiqh works — a text so dense that finishing it traditionally marks the end of a student's formal study of legal theory. He also compiled Tabaqat al-Shafi'iyyah al-Kubra, one of the major biographical dictionaries of Shafi'i scholars still used as a historical reference today.
