Ali Khalid al-Sharbaji al-Dimashqi al-Dari, born in Daraya near Damascus in 1928, memorized the Qur'an as a child and studied under some of twentieth-century Damascus's leading scholars, including Hasan Habannakah al-Maydani. He is the third co-author, alongside Mustafa al-Khinn and Mustafa al-Bugha, of al-Fiqh al-Manhaji — the widely used intermediate Shafi'i textbook based on Mughni al-Muhtaj that has introduced generations of Arabic-speaking students to evidence-based fiqh. He is noted for his ability to present fiqh in clear, contemporary language, and separately authored works on Hanbali and Maliki fiqh as well.
