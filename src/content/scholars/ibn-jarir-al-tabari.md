Ibn Jarir al-Tabari produced two monumental works that remain foundational reference points in their fields nearly eleven centuries later: his tafsir, which systematically gathers and evaluates the exegetical opinions of the earliest generations of Muslims, and his history, one of the primary sources for the early Islamic period. His independence of thought was significant enough that he is sometimes considered the founder of a short-lived school of legal reasoning in his own right.
