Muhammad ibn Qasim al-Ghazzi wrote Fath al-Qarib as a direct, unadorned explanation of Abu Shuja's primer — deliberately avoiding extended debate or evidentiary detail in favor of simply making sure the beginning student understands what each line of the matn is actually saying. Its restraint is exactly why it has remained the standard first commentary for centuries.
