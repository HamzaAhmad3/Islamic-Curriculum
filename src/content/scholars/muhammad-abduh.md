Muhammad Abduh served as Grand Mufti of Egypt and became one of the central figures of the late 19th-century Islamic reform movement, arguing for a re-engagement with the Qur'an and Sunnah alongside a critical openness to modern conditions. He began Tafsir al-Manar as a lecture series; his student Rashid Rida recorded, completed, and published it after Abduh's death.
