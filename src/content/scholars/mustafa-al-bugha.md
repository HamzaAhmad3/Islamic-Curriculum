Mustafa al-Bugha taught for decades in Damascus and became known for a consistent editorial approach: taking dense classical texts and re-presenting them with their underlying evidences made explicit, in modern academic Arabic accessible to students without a fully traditional training. Al-Fiqh al-Manhaji, co-written with Mustafa al-Khinn and Ali al-Sharbaji, remains one of the most widely used intermediate fiqh textbooks in the Arabic-speaking world.
