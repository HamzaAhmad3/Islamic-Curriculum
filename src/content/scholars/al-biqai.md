Al-Biqa'i built his entire tafsir around a single organizing question most exegetes treat only in passing: why does the Qur'an arrange its verses and chapters in the order it does? Nadhm al-Durar systematically argues for the coherence and interrelation (tanasub) of the text's structure, a genuinely distinctive angle within the broader tafsir tradition.
