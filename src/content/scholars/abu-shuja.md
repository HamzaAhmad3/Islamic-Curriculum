Ahmad ibn al-Husayn al-Isfahani, known as Abu Shuja', served as a judge and wrote a short legal primer — properly titled al-Ghayah wa al-Taqrib — that would go on to become one of the most heavily commented-upon texts in Islamic legal history. Its brevity and clear structure made it a natural entry point for beginners, and the resulting tradition of commentary, from Fath al-Qarib through al-Iqna' and beyond, effectively maps the entire progression from beginner to advanced Shafi'i fiqh.
