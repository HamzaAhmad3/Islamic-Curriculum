Muhammad Hasan Hitu was born in Damascus in 1943 into a family known for its attention to the religious sciences, and traveled to Cairo to study at al-Azhar despite considerable financial hardship, eventually earning his doctorate in Usul al-Fiqh there. He went on to teach at universities across several countries and founded Imam al-Shafi'i University in Indonesia. His al-Khulasah fi Usul al-Fiqh is valued for doing something genuinely difficult well: presenting traditional Shafi'i Usul al-Fiqh methodology in clear, accessible modern Arabic without sacrificing the discipline's underlying logical structure. He passed away in Kuwait in February 2026 at the age of 83, widely mourned across the Muslim world as one of the leading Usul al-Fiqh scholars of his generation.
