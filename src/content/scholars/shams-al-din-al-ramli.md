Muhammad Shams al-Din al-Ramli inherited both a family scholarly tradition and, effectively, half of the later Shafi'i school's final legal authority. His father Shihab al-Din al-Ramli had already begun a major commentary project (Fath al-Rahman) on Safwat al-Zubad; Shams al-Din completed and built on this foundation across his career, culminating in Nihayat al-Muhtaj, his monumental commentary on Minhaj al-Talibin. Where his rulings diverge from Ibn Hajar al-Haytami's, students in Egypt and Syria are generally taught to follow al-Ramli.
