Ibn 'Ashur served as Shaykh al-Islam of Tunisia and produced, in al-Tahrir wa al-Tanwir, one of the most significant tafsirs of the 20th century — distinguished by close attention to the Qur'an's rhetorical and literary structure alongside its legal and theological content. His separate work on maqasid al-shari'ah has also been highly influential in modern Islamic legal theory.
