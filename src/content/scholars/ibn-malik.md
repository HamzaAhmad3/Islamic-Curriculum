Jamal al-Din Ibn Malik left al-Andalus for Damascus, where he produced the Alfiyyah — a thousand-line versification of Arabic grammar so thorough, and so memorable in verse form, that it became the effective capstone of the traditional Nahw curriculum across the entire Muslim world. Centuries of subsequent grammarians, most famously Ibn 'Aqil and Ibn Hisham, built their own reputations partly on commentaries explaining Ibn Malik's compressed verses.
