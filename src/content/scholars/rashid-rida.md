Rashid Rida moved from Lebanon to Egypt specifically to study with Muhammad Abduh, and after Abduh's death carried the reform project forward — recording, expanding, and publishing Tafsir al-Manar based on his teacher's lectures, and running the influential journal al-Manar that gave the broader movement its name.
