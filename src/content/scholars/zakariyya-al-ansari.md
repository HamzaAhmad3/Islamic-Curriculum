Zakariyya al-Ansari rose from poverty in the Egyptian countryside to become Chief Judge of Egypt and one of the most prolific and widely taught scholars of his era, reportedly living past the age of one hundred. He personally abridged Minhaj al-Talibin into his own primer, Manhaj al-Tullab, then wrote several commentaries connecting his own work back to al-Nawawi's original text. His most lasting legacy may be pedagogical rather than textual: both Ibn Hajar al-Haytami and Shams al-Din al-Ramli studied under him before going on to become the two authorities whose commentaries later defined the school's relied-upon positions.
